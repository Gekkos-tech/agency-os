# Aesthetic Registers — Prompt Vocabulary

Pick exactly ONE register per brand. Copy 5–10 keywords from its vocabulary into every prompt of the kit. Never mix vocabularies across registers within one brand.

Each register lists: when to use it, prompt vocabulary, palette logic, type feel, mockup bias, and what to explicitly forbid.

---

## 1. Minimal

**Use for:** design studios, architecture, productivity tools, premium services that sell clarity.

**Vocabulary:** `swiss design`, `grid-based layout`, `generous negative space`, `flat graphic design`, `monochrome`, `precise geometry`, `hairline rules`, `understated`, `gallery presentation`, `off-white paper background`, `subtle paper texture`.

**Palette logic:** near-black (#111111 range) + warm off-white (#F5F2EC range) + at most one muted accent. Often no accent at all.

**Type feel:** neo-grotesque sans, tight tracking, generous size contrast between wordmark and labels. Lowercase or all-caps, never mixed decorative styles.

**Mockup bias:** letterhead, business card, thin book spine, small signage plate.

**Forbid:** gradients, shadows, textures heavier than paper grain, more than one accent color, decorative icons.

---

## 2. Cinematic

**Use for:** film/media brands, ambitious startups wanting drama, event brands, storytelling products.

**Vocabulary:** `cinematic lighting`, `volumetric light`, `anamorphic feel`, `deep shadows`, `atmospheric haze`, `moody`, `film still quality`, `dramatic scale`, `low-key lighting`, `35mm grain`.

**Palette logic:** very dark base (#0B0E12 range), one luminous accent used like a light source (amber, teal, signal red). Whites reserved for type.

**Type feel:** condensed or extended sans in caps, wide letterspacing, small and confident — title-card energy, not poster shouting.

**Mockup bias:** large signage in dark environments, title cards, backlit panels, dark hardcover.

**Forbid:** flat pastel backgrounds, playful rounded type, busy multi-accent palettes, daylight product-shot lighting.

---

## 3. Editorial

**Use for:** publications, agencies, fashion-adjacent brands, cultural institutions, newsletters.

**Vocabulary:** `editorial layout`, `magazine spread`, `serif display typography`, `asymmetric grid`, `art direction`, `print quality`, `ink on uncoated paper`, `column structure`, `pull-quote scale contrast`, `photographic duotone`.

**Palette logic:** paper white + ink black as the core; one confident accent (oxblood, cobalt, mustard) used sparingly like a highlighter.

**Type feel:** high-contrast serif display paired with a plain text sans. Big/small contrast is the signature — one huge word, small everything else.

**Mockup bias:** magazine cover, folded broadsheet, tote, poster on a wall.

**Forbid:** techy glows, neon, glassmorphism, centered-everything symmetry.

---

## 4. Dark-tech

**Use for:** developer tools, infrastructure, security, AI products, fintech backends.

**Vocabulary:** `dark UI aesthetic`, `near-black background`, `monospaced accents`, `terminal green` (or chosen accent), `precise thin lines`, `blueprint feel`, `technical diagram linework`, `subtle glow only on the accent`, `matte dark surfaces`, `engineered`.

**Palette logic:** #0A0A0C-range background, desaturated gray text tones, ONE saturated accent (electric green, cyan, violet, or amber). The accent is the brand.

**Type feel:** clean grotesque for the wordmark + monospace for labels/hex codes. Lowercase wordmarks read as native here.

**Mockup bias:** app icon, dark laptop/terminal screen, black keycap, badge/lanyard, dark hardware faceplate.

**Forbid:** light backgrounds, multiple neon colors at once, cyberpunk clutter, circuit-board clichés, hooded-hacker imagery.

---

## 5. Luxury

**Use for:** fashion, jewelry, hospitality, premium spirits, high-end real estate, private services.

**Vocabulary:** `understated luxury`, `embossed`, `blind deboss`, `gold foil detail` (sparing), `heavy cotton paper`, `soft studio lighting`, `shallow depth of field`, `tactile materials`, `monogram`, `timeless`, `quiet elegance`.

**Palette logic:** cream/ivory or deep charcoal base; metallics (gold, brass) as material, not as flat color; optionally one deep tone (forest, bordeaux, navy).

**Type feel:** refined serif or elegant extended sans with wide letterspacing, all-caps wordmark, tiny supporting text. A monogram or crest-like mark often carries the identity.

**Mockup bias:** rigid box with foil, hangtag on ribbon, embossed card, glass bottle, hotel key sleeve.

**Forbid:** bright saturated accents, playful shapes, heavy borders, anything that says "discount" — big text, badges, starbursts.

---

## 6. Playful-consumer

**Use for:** consumer apps, food & beverage, kids/family products, lifestyle brands, social products.

**Vocabulary:** `bold flat colors`, `rounded geometry`, `friendly mascot-adjacent mark` (only if concept calls for it), `chunky type`, `sticker-like elements`, `cheerful`, `high color confidence`, `smooth vector shapes`, `soft shadows`, `energetic but tidy`.

**Palette logic:** 2–3 saturated hues that genuinely harmonize (state hexes!), plus a cream or white ground. Confidence, not rainbow: still cap at one dominant + supporting colors.

**Type feel:** rounded or chunky geometric sans, mixed-case, big friendly wordmark. Avoid novelty display fonts — chunk + roundness does the work.

**Mockup bias:** app icon, cup/can, sticker sheet, phone case, delivery bag.

**Forbid:** clip-art randomness, comic sans energy, more than 3 hues, gradients on the wordmark, clutter — playful still needs negative space.

---

## Cross-register constants

Whatever the register:

- End every prompt with the restraint clause from SKILL.md.
- Specify every visible word; keep total in-image text under ~15 words per board.
- Repeat the same BRAND CONCEPT block and hex list in every prompt of the set.
- If a generation drifts register (e.g. luxury board comes back neon), fix the prompt vocabulary — do not "average" registers to compensate.
