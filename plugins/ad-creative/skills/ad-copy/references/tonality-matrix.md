# Tonality Matrix (DE + EN)

Reference for the ad-copy skill. © GEKKOS Tech, MIT License.

Three dimensions position every line of copy:

1. **Brand voice** — three sliders: serious ↔ playful · premium ↔ accessible · expert ↔ peer.
2. **Platform norms** — what the surrounding feed sounds like; copy that ignores platform register reads as an intruder.
3. **Funnel stage** — cold needs clarity and low commitment; hot needs specificity and reassurance.

Pick ONE cell before writing. State it in the deliverable (e.g. "Voice: expert-premium-serious × LinkedIn × warm").

## Platform norms (baseline register per channel)

| Platform | Register | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | professional-peer, evidence-friendly | Numbers and outcomes work; hype gets punished. Sie acceptable in DE. |
| Meta (FB/IG) | conversational, benefit-forward | First ~90 chars carry the load; emoji sparingly, DE default Du. |
| TikTok / Reels | native-casual, spoken-word | Copy sounds like a person, not a brand; Sie is almost always wrong. |
| Google Search | intent-matching, literal | Mirror the query; no wit, no curiosity gaps. |
| Display / Banner | 5-word clarity | One benefit, one CTA; register from brand, not platform. |
| Email | one-to-one, direct | Subject = curiosity or benefit system; body matches brand slider. |

## The matrix

Cells = brand voice archetype × funnel stage, with platform adjustments noted. Each cell: one EN and one DE example line (headline or primary text opener). Product placeholder: a scheduling/booking tool.

### Voice A: Expert · Premium · Serious (e.g. B2B enterprise, finance, medical)

| Stage | EN example | DE example |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | *"Scheduling errors cost clinics 11 hours a week. There is a fix."* | *"Terminfehler kosten Praxen 11 Stunden pro Woche. Es geht anders."* (Sie implied) |
| Warm | *"See why 400 practices standardized on one scheduling system."* | *"Erfahren Sie, warum 400 Praxen auf ein System umgestiegen sind."* |
| Hot | *"Your implementation plan is ready. 30-day rollout, guided."* | *"Ihr Einführungsplan steht. Rollout in 30 Tagen, mit Begleitung."* |

Platform notes: native on LinkedIn and search; on Meta, soften syntax but keep authority; avoid TikTok-native slang entirely.

### Voice B: Expert · Accessible · Peer (e.g. B2B SaaS for SMB/startups)

| Stage | EN example | DE example |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | *"Still scheduling over email ping-pong? There's a faster way."* | *"Terminfindung per E-Mail-Ping-Pong? Geht auch in 30 Sekunden."* (Du) |
| Warm | *"Compare it to your current setup — takes 5 minutes, might save 5 hours."* | *"Vergleich dein aktuelles Setup – 5 Minuten, die 5 Stunden sparen können."* |
| Hot | *"Your trial data carries over. Pick a plan, keep working."* | *"Deine Testdaten bleiben erhalten. Plan wählen, weiterarbeiten."* |

Platform notes: the default register for Meta + LinkedIn B2B; DE uses Du for startup/tech audiences.

### Voice C: Playful · Accessible · Peer (e.g. consumer apps, D2C, lifestyle)

| Stage | EN example | DE example |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | *"Your calendar called. It wants a manager."* | *"Dein Kalender hätte da mal eine Bitte: Hilfe."* |
| Warm | *"You've seen us around. Time for a first date — it's free."* | *"Du kennst uns schon. Zeit für ein erstes Date – kostet nichts."* |
| Hot | *"That plan you looked at? Still 30% off. Still perfect for you."* | *"Der Plan von neulich? Immer noch 30 % günstiger. Immer noch deiner."* |

Platform notes: native on TikTok/Reels/Meta; tone down wordplay on search (intent mismatch); on LinkedIn, keep the warmth but drop the jokes.

### Voice D: Premium · Serious · Aspirational (e.g. luxury, high-end services)

| Stage | EN example | DE example |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | *"Time is the only luxury you can't buy back. Guard it."* | *"Zeit ist der einzige Luxus, den man nicht zurückkauft. Schützen Sie ihn."* |
| Warm | *"For those who plan their year, not their week."* | *"Für alle, die in Jahren planen, nicht in Wochen."* |
| Hot | *"Your private onboarding awaits. One conversation, everything arranged."* | *"Ihr persönliches Onboarding wartet. Ein Gespräch, alles geregelt."* |

Platform notes: sparse copy, no urgency theatrics, no discount language in the headline; CTAs invitation-styled ("Request access" / "Zugang anfragen").

## Using the matrix

1. Locate the brand on the three voice sliders → pick the closest archetype row (A–D). If between two, name the blend ("B with A's proof density").
2. Cross with funnel stage → the cell gives the register and example pattern; write fresh lines in that register, never reuse example lines verbatim.
3. Apply the platform adjustment note last — platform bends syntax and length, voice stays constant.
4. Consistency check: read all assets of one campaign in sequence; if two lines sound like different companies, re-align to the chosen cell.

## Register red flags

- Voice A copy with exclamation marks or emoji → premium-serious brands assert, they don't shout.
- Voice C copy on Google Search → jokes don't match query intent; switch to literal benefit.
- Du/Sie drift between headline and body → hard error, fix before delivery.
- English idioms translated literally into German ("Das ist ein Gamechanger für dich!") → rewrite natively or keep the anglicism only if the brand voice is documented as denglisch-friendly.
